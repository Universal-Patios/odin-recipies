Esquites

This recipe will show you how to make one of Mexicos' favorite street foods. Its a creamy, delicious, and simple treat that can be eaten as a side dish to a meal or eaten by itself as a snack

Ingredients

  • 2 medium sized cobs of corn
  • About 1.5 tablespoons of dried epazote(3 tablespoons of fresh
  • 1/2 of a medium sized white onion, chopped
  • 2 cloved of garlic, minced
  • 2 cups of water
  • Salt
  • 1 lime
  • Mayonaise, crumbled cheese, tajin(chili powder)

Instructions

  1. First take the kernals off of the corn with a knife or other kitchen utensil
  2. Next, chop the onion and garlic
  3. Place the corn, garlic, onion, and epazote into a medium cooking pot and boil for 20-30 mins (until the corn is soft). Replace some water if it gets low. But not too much! You dont want it to be too watery
  4. When the corn is cooked add a little salt to taste and serve into cups
  5. Add a little mayonaise, lime juice, cheese, and tajin to the cup and mix in. Everyone likes different amounts of each so experiment with what you like the best!

You can add things like shredded chicken ontop as well. Or mix some salsa verde inside to give it a little extra flavor!
