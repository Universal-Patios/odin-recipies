Sheredded Chicken

This is a very simple recipe for shredded chicken. The left over broth (consome) can and should be saved and used for future soups or salsa

Ingredients

  • One whole chicken breast
  • 2 bay leaves
  • one clove of garlic
  • 1/4 of a meduim white onion
  • 4 cups of water
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Place everything but the salt in a small/medium sized cooking pot. Make sure its small enough that the whole chicken is covered in water
  2. Bring water to a boil and cook for 30 minutes. Until the inside of the chicken is no longer pink (scoop off any of the white foam that floats the top)
  3. Add about a 1/4-1/2 teaspoon of salt (you can add more afterwards if you like)
  4. When the chicken is cooked remove it from the water and let cool for a few mintues
  5. Dont dump the water! You can save the broth in a jar and use it for any dishes that call for it
  6. Once the chicken has cooled gently rip it in half and start shredding each piece into tiny strips. You will be able to see there is a 'grain" to the chicken. Make sure to shred the chicken with "the grain" in order to get nice thin strips

Thats it! very simple and fast to make. You can use this as a topping for chilaquiles, or with your esquites. As well, you can use it with salsa verde to make dishes like enchilads!
